Bernie Pope

Computer scientist and bioinformatician

Overview

I am a computer scientist and bioinformatician, applying scalable and robust computational methodologies to key challenges in human health, particularly in genomics and cancer.

Roles

  • Professor in the Faculty of Medicine, Dentistry and Health Sciences at The University of Melbourne, Australia
  • Bioinformatics Lead, Melbourne Bioinformatics, The University of Melbourne, Australia
  • Associate Director (Human Genome Informatics), Australian BioCommons

Other Affiliations and Duties

  • Adjunct Associate Professor (Research), Department of Medicine, Central Clinical School, Faculty of Medicine Nursing and Health Sciences, Monash University, Australia
  • Honorary Clinical Senior Fellow, Department of Surgery (Royal Melbourne Hospital), Melbourne Medical School, Faculty of Medicine, Dentistry and Health Sciences, The University of Melbourne, Australia
  • Honorary Adjunct Senior Lecturer, Department of Clinical Pathology, Melbourne Medical School, Faculty of Medicine, Dentistry and Health Sciences, The University of Melbourne, Australia
  • Honorary Fellow, School of Computing and Information Systems, The University of Melbourne, Australia
  • Associate Member, Melbourne Integrative Genomics, The University of Melbourne, Australia
  • Co-lead of the Global Alliance for Genomics & Health (GA4GH) Cancer Community
  • Co-chair of the Technical Working Group and Steering Committee member of the Pan Prostate Cancer Group (PPCG)
